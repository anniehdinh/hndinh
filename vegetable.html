Stir-fried Green Beans

This dish features fresh green beans quickly stir-fried with garlic and a savory sauce. The green beans remain crisp and vibrant, coated with a light, flavorful glaze. It's typically served as a side dish alongside rice and other main courses. Simple, healthy, and quick to prepare.

Serves Prep Cook Total Time
4 servings 10 minutes 10 minutes 20 minutes

Ingredients

  • 12 oz green beans, trimmed
  • 1 tbsp cooking oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp onion, minced (optional)
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce (optional)
  • Sesame seed for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Trim the ends of the green beans. I'd recommend using either a knife to line them up and slice off the ends or a pair of scissors for a quick snip.
  2. Blanch the green beans. Bring a large saucepan of water to boil with 1 tsp of salt. Add in the green beans, cook for about 4 minutes (they will turn bright green), remove from heat and drain. Rinse them under cold water and shake off excess water.
  3. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium high heat. Add minced garlic, minced onion (optional) and stir for about 1 minute or until fragrant.
  4. Add in the green beans, soy sauce, sugar and oyster sauce (optional). Stir fry for another minute or until everything is evenly coated and aromatic.
  5. Transfer the green beans to a serving plate. Garnish with sesame seeds (optional), and enjoy hot as a side dish.
