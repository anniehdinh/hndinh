Vietnamese Stir-Fried Morning Glory / Rau Muống Xào Tỏi

"Rau muống" is a popular leafy green vegetable in Vietnamese cuisine, often stir-fried with garlic for a simple yet flavorful side dish. This recipe highlights the fresh, crisp texture of morning glory combined with the aromatic punch of garlic and a touch of savory seasoning. It's a quick and healthy addition to any meal, perfect for balancing richer dishes. Simple, flavorful, and quick to prepare. It's a perfect meal for any day of the week.

Serves Prep Cook Total Time
4 servings 20 minutes 10 minutes 30 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 lb beef sirloin or tenderloin, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1 head of lettuce or watercress, for serving
  • Sliced tomatoes and cucumbers, for garnish
  • Steamed jasmine rice, for serving

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, combine soy sauce, oyster sauce, fish sauce, sugar, and black pepper. Mix well and set aside.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over high heat. Add minced garlic and sauté until fragrant, about 30 seconds.
  3. Add the beef cubes to the skillet in a single layer. Let them sear without moving for about 2 minutes to develop a nice crust.
  4. Toss the beef cubes and continue to cook for another 2-3 minutes until they are browned on all sides but still medium-rare inside. Adjust cooking time based on your preferred doneness.
  5. Pour the sauce mixture over the beef and add water. Stir well to coat the beef evenly. Cook for another 1-2 minutes until the sauce thickens slightly.
  6. Remove from heat and let the beef rest for a few minutes.
  7. Serve the shaking beef over a bed of lettuce or watercress, garnished with sliced tomatoes and cucumbers. Accompany with steamed jasmine rice.
