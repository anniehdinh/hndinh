Vietnamese Shaking Beef / Bo Luc Lac

"Bo" means beef, and "luc lac" means shaking, together we have shaking beef. This classic Vietnamese dish features tender cubes of beef that are seared over high heat and tossed in a savory sauce. The beef is juicy, and coated with a delightful mix of flavors from garlic, soy sauce, and a hint of sweetness. It's typically served over a bed of watercress or lettuce, sliced tomatoes and/or cucumber, and with a side of steamed rice. Simple, flavorful, and quick to prepare. It's a perfect meal for any day of the week.

Serves Prep Cook Total Time
4 servings 30 minutes 10 minutes 40 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 lb beef sirloin or tenderloin, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 3 tbsp neutral oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, grated
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 2 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce
  • 2 tsp light soy sauce
  • 1 tsp cornstarch
  • 1/2 tsp dark soy sauce
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1 medium yellow onion, cut into 2 pieces
  • 1 head of lettuce or watercress, for serving
  • Sliced tomatoes and cucumbers, for garnish
  • Steamed jasmine rice, for serving

Instructions

  1. In a medium bowl, add the beef cubes, oyster sauce, brown sugar, light soy sauce, fish sauce, grated garlic, cornstarch, ground black pepper, and neutral oil. Mix well. Cover it, and let it marinate in the refrigerator for 30 minutes or overnight for best results.
  2. While the beef is marinating, prepare your lettuce, sliced cucumber and sliced tomatoes. On a serving platter, add a bed of lettuce or watercress. Arrange sliced cucumbers and tomatoes on either side of the plate.
  3. Heat a wok or skillet over medium-high heat until it's hot. Add 1 tablespoon of oil, and add the beef cubes to the wok in a single layer. Let the beef sear without moving for about 2 minutes to develop a nice crust.
  4. Toss the beef cubes and continue to cook for another 2-3 minutes until they are browned on all sides but still medium-rare inside. Adjust cooking time based on your preferred doneness then transfer the beef to a bowl.
  5. Using the same wok, add the onions and another 1/2 tablespoon of oil. Cook for 2 minutes.
  6. Add the beef back to the wok, along with dark soy sauce and butter. Give everything a toss to combine, allowing the sauce to coat the beef and onions evenly.
  7. Transfer the beef to your prepared serving platter, on top of your bed of lettuce. Enjoy with steamed rice.
