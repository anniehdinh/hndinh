Seafood Dish

Garlic Butter Shrimp

This simple yet delicious dish features succulent shrimp sautéed in a rich garlic butter sauce. The shrimp are tender and flavorful, coated in a luscious blend of butter, garlic, and a hint of lemon. It's a quick, easy, tasty dish perfect for serving with steamed rice.

Serves Prep Cook Total Time
4 servings 15 minutes 10 minutes 25 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 lb shrimp (deveined, shell-on)
  • 1 stick unsalted butter
  • 1/3 cup garlic, chopped
  • 4 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 3 tbsp flour
  • 3 tbsp corn starch
  • 1 tbsp paprika
  • 1 lemon
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/4 tsp cayenne pepper (optional)
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce (optional)
  • 1/4 tsp onion powder (optional)
  • Parsley for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Clean and devein the shrimps but leave shells on.
  2. Pat the shrimp completely dry with paper towel. The drier they are, the crispier they'll cook.
  3. In a dry bowl, combine all-purpose flour, cornstarch, onion powder (optional), paprika, salt, and pepper. Add in the shrimp and toss until evenly coated. Set aside.
  4. Prepare the aromatics. Chop the garlic and cut the lemon in half.
  5. Heat a large sauté pan over medium heat and add in oil. Once the oil is hot, add in the shrimp in a single layer. Pan-fry the shrimp for 1-2 minutes on each side, until shrimp is just cooked through. Remove and set aside.
  6. In the same pan, add in butter, chopped garlic, cayenne pepper (optional), oyster sauce (optional), garlic powder and juice from half a lemon. Stir for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  7. Return the shrimp to the pan and toss well to coat them evenly in the sauce and then remove from heat.
  8. Plate the shrimp, garnish with chopped parsley and fresh lemon wedges on the side. Enjoy with steamed rice.
